Distortion

There's always going to be distortion in our lives. People like to refer to that as 'perspective', and that's a nice way to think about it, but really our brain processes fact and distorts it to fit our own narrative. It's takes training yourself to be objective and practical, to prevent a distortion on information. Now, it's not always bad, since bringing a new perspective to a topic can shake things up, but when discussing statistics, graphs, and data there's very little room allowed for personal interpretations. Can this thought process be considered 'cold?' Yes.

Memory

With the distortion comes our memory. Our memory is a great contributor to the distortion of information. After a certain number of years, our memory can only recall about almost half of the information from a memory correctly. But most of the time it's not about accuracy. Most of the time, if the memory is good, our brain will expell the nonsense information to retain the good information, and then in other, more extreme cases our brains will rewrite themselves to protect us from traumatic memories to ensure our emotional survival.
