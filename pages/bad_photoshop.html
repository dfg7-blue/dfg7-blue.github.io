Project Notes

For this project, we were tasked with taking photo's of odd still lives to then import into Adobe Photoshop, and edit them in a similar style to Lucas Blalock's work. In following his work method, I used a combination of the eraser tool, layer masking, the clone stamp tool, and some selection and drawing tools to recreate the surreal and unnatural photoshopped still lives that Blalock makes. The title of this project is 'Bad Photoshop' because the mistakes are made clear and visable, adding to the work by leaving parts of the process in the final work. It's not clean, nor is it meant to be.

Process Documentation

This is one of the the source images that I took to use for the Bad Photoshop project. I thought that the bright yellow would make for a good source of manipulation in Photoshop. Since I planned on using quite a few of the tools in the program, I wanted to keep my sources simple, as to not overwhelm myself or the viewer.
What I ended up doing was focusing on the arms, using the clone stamp and selection tools to create several arms on each side, and then cleaning up the connection areas with the eraser tool. I had the most fun with this image, although I'm not all that happy with the outcome.

Reflection

To be honest, I did not enjoy this project. I am someone who agonizes over balanced and clean work, so even though I had fun with the arms in the image it's clear I didn't care much for the final product. The yellow, as I figured, was fun to mess around with, though I wish the bottom stripes were perfect instead of hand drawn. I'm not a fan of Lucas Blalock's work, or most of the odd photoshopped still lives that were used as examples. It's safe to say this style of work is not for me, but that's fine. If anything, it was at least a good exersize to introduce me to the several tools available to me in Photoshop.
