Project Notes

For this project, we were tasked with creating two perfect layer masks in Adobe Photoshop, following the style of the artist John Baldessari. Our two images were to be black-and-white photos from the 1950s, 1960s, or 1970s. Then, we were asked to select an object or person in the photo to mask with a solid color, while leaving the rest of the black-and-white background intact. This was done with a mask layer, the selection and paint brush tool, as well as the paint bucket fill tool.

Process Documentation

This is one of the two images I found to use for his project, and I liked the results of this image better than the second one. I chose the middle women in the lighter colored outfit for my masked colored object. I made a solid color layer of bright pink, which I thought would contrast nicely against the black-and-white image. I then added a layer mask to the solid pink layer, and then with the selection tool, I selected the woman to then mask. I had a bit of difficulty at the start figuring out which layer I had to mask and in what order, but I eventually figured it out.

Reflection

As I stated, the final image was my favorite of the two I created, due to the color choice and the accidental details of the work. I like how the other woman is interacting with the solid colored figure, and that her shoes aren't masked. For me, this begs the question of if she were to drop down and slip on the shoes, would the shoes turn pink or will she be pink figure with real 3D shoes. In the end, this project allowed me to mess with masking, while keeping the creation process simple enough that I wasn't overwhelmed.
