PIXELATED

  • Back to Main Page

This project required pulling images from the internet and resizing the image to a specified limited pixel amount. Each pixel amount--16px, 32px, 64px, and 128px--was to have two images each, making a total of eight pixelated images. The resulting images was to resemble, or at least capture the original image in it's essense or content in a reduced state of image quality.

Project Notes

For this project, I had not yet received my temporary student Photoshop liscence, so I created this project in Clip Studio. Although I did complete the project on time and to satisfaction, the process was a bit...janky. Clip Studio is not meant to be used in such a way, so it was a bit of a back and forth the whole way through.

Process Documentation

This is the original that I extraced my choice image from for one of the the 32 pixel images. I was drawn to the bright, crips colors of the yellow flower and green bird, thinking that they would communicate well at the reduced pixel amount. Looking at the surrounding details, I can see that this photo has either been heavily edited already, or been processed by AI. Seems fitting that it would go through another round of transformation by my hands.

This was one of the first images I made during the project, and since I was still trying to work out the correct formating of the pixelated images, I ended up with this hot mess. I was going back and forth between pixel amount and image size, since they were two different things that couldn't be the same in Clip Studio. So, what I ended up doing was created the desired pixelated image, take a screen shot with my canvas zoomed in to the max, and then created a new dcument to then size the image without messing with the desired pixel effect. Very backwards and not at all what our process was supposed to be, but that is what I had to do at the time. If there's a will there's a way.

Reflection

I will say, I learned quite a bit about the history of the pixel. I didn't learn much else, outside of the fact that Clip Studio is not meant for this type of work process in anyway shape or form. Which is fine, seeing as it's a program meant for comic illustration and digital art. Outside of the actual project topic, I learned quite a bit about how far I'm willing to go to make something work, to the point where all I have left is a saved file of a resized screenshot of a tiny pixelated image.
