Project Notes

This project takes us to p5.js, a coding website that specializes in images, both interactive and still. It's a very flexible way of coding, and with that in mind the project asked for a landscape, and a self portrait to be made in p5.js using shapes and colors. For the landscape, it required at least twelve different shapes, while the self portrait didn't have a minimum. These projects are called 'sketches', and despite the limiting detail were to represent the subject requested.

Process Documentation

The first one I worked on was the landscape, thinking it was going to be the more difficult one with the minimum shape requirement. I started with the trees, creating them at different sizes to create the illusion of depth, and included grass, a mountain in the back so the sky wasn't so empty, and a sliver of a river. I ended up using exactly twelve shapes for this assignment.

Although I assumed the landscape would be the more difficult of the two, I was wrong. The self portrait was tedious, with each shape having to be perfectly sized and place to make the portrait look somewhat believable to the human eye. I kept most of the shapes lineless, only using lines around the eyes and the bottom of the opened mouth.

Reflection

Upon reflection, I'm not thrilled with p5.js. I'm sure many people would hav great fun creating clever projects in the coding software, but I found it overly tedious for a mediocure product. But, this mostly has to do with the fact that I am a fine artist, not a coder. The portrait looks...maybe like me, but who is to argue with the accuracy of a couple of ovals and well placed triangles? Overall, the results are cute, but not something I'm itching to mess with again.
