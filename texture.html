The Failure of AI Art

Although I had to research what the term 'aesthetic failure' actually meant, I find it fits the climate of AI generated art. In what can be used in wonderful ways outside of art, has been corrupted by those unable to put in the effort to actually learn a skill. It is not art. It is a series of code that generates pixles based off of a word prompt. It is soulless, and is the very epitome of a true artists worst nightmare, it is vile. If I were to ask what composition they used, what medium, what drawing program, what type of brush, what color pallete and why, what element of art did they focus on--they have nothing to say. Art isn't meant to be acessable. It isn't a telephone you can use for a quarter. It is a skill that is developed, earned. Not stolen and distorted through the use of a coded computer intelligence program.

Distortion

This distortion placed upon the use of AI generated art, photos and videos is disgusting. It is viewed by a section of the masses as a 'short-cut' into art, while companies view it as a way to save money and generate posters and even entire ads with AI. Both of these aren't true. The job crises is only going to get worse the further this distortion goes, for all jobs, really. Actors won't be hired for ads anymore, marketers won't be hired for logo designs and marketing strategies, and job recruiters are already being affected with the rise of AI generated job interviews. Creatively, companies won't hire artists to design illustrations, coloring books, animate series, and much more.
